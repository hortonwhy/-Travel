Kagoshima Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Kagoshima Prefecture is located on the island of Kyushu and the Ryukyu Islands. It is distinguished by two peninsulas on the southern tip of Japan. Historically, Kagoshima Prefecture was one of the most important Japanese domains of the Edo period and the Meiji Restoration where ruling took place from the Kagoshima Castle. It also served as an important naval base in the 20th century. Kagoshima is home to numerous active volcanoes, in some areas it is advised to carry an umbrella to make sure you don’t get covered with ash. Kagoshima Prefecture plays an imperative role as both an agricultural and industrial hotspot for its expansive port system.
