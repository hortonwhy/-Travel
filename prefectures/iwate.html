Iwate Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Iwate Prefecture is located in the Tohoku region of Honshu. It is the second largest prefecture. Its jagged coastline is along Japan’s Pacific Ocean Coast and beholds the eastern most point of Honshu, Cape Todo. Iwate shares the highest peaks of the Ou Mountains, the longest mountain range in Japan. Iwate has a violent history of hundreds-of-years of bloodshed between the Emishi natives and Japanese frontiersmen fighting for control of the region as well as a devil who was said to harass and torment the locals. After the Boshin Civil War in 1876, Iwate Prefecture was finally created. Within the geography are active volcanoes, hot springs, and vast mineral deposits. On March 11, 2011, a massive magnitude 9.0 earthquake followed by a Tsunami devastated the prefecture. (Source: Iwate Official Site)
