Kyoto Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Kyoto Prefecture is located in the Kansai region of Honshu along the Sea of Japan and centralized around the Imperial Capital of Japan, Kyoto. It is known to be a highly populated region. Many original streets, houses, stones, temples, and shrines from the late 700;s can still be found in Kyoto. These artifacts have survived violent eras such as the Onin Civil War and the Age of Warring Feudal Lords. Kyoto Prefecture was also relatively spared in World War II because it was a sought after headquarters for the U.S. Sixth Army. Also, Kyoto is a world leader of technology and manufacturing. Not to mention Kyoto produces 13% of the domestic sake and green tea. Kyoto Prefecture is considered to be Japan’s cultural epicenter.
