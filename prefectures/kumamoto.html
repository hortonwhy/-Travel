Kumamoto Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Kumamoto Prefecture is located on the island of Kyushu. It is home to Mount Aso, the largest active volcano in Japan standing at 5223 feet above sea level. Historically speaking, Kumamoto is part of the abolition of the feudal system. Among its historical artifacts, Kumamoto Prefecture boasts the largest stone aqueduct in Japan and numerous buildings from the Meiji restoration.
