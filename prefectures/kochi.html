Kochi Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Kochi Prefecture is located on the island of Shikoku and borders Japan’s pacific coastline creating a large bay. It is home to Japan’s most intact castle, Kochi Castle and the Shimanto River, one of Japan’s famously undammed rivers. Kochi is also the birthplace of Sakamoto Ryoma, a well-known instigator of the Meiji Restoration. Most of the prefecture is mountainous and contains many beautiful rivers, as well as 18 exquisite Prefectural Natural Parks. Kochi is also famous for katsuo no tataki and sawachi which includes large, informal feasts. (Source: Kochi Official Site)
