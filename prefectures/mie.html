Mie Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Mie Prefecture is located in the Kansai region of Honshu and beholds the beautiful delta of the Kiso Three Rivers. Historically speaking, it is home to some of the eldest and holiest sites in Shinto, or the traditional religion of Japan. Furthermore, there was even human evidence found dating back more than 10,000 years! Mie is still scarred by the 1959 Ise-wan Typhoon, the strongest typhoon to ever hit Japan where the prefecture was critically damaged. A rather large 36% of the total area of the prefecture consists of Natural Parks which feature magnificent coastlines and thick forests. Mie Prefecture is considered to be a line between east and west Japan in a traditional sense.
