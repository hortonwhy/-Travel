Ishikawa Prefecture

Historical BackGround/Context

Ishikawa Prefecture is located in the Chubu region of Honshu. It sits on the coast of the Sea of Japan and forms a majority of the Toyama Bay, one of the largest bays in Japan. At one point Ishikawa Prefecture was dominated by some of the wealthiest han of the Japanese feudal era. Now, many historical artifacts can be seen such as the Kanazawa Castle. Also, Ishikawa is a very cultural Prefecture with an abundance of art, museums, and festivals. Among the art forms include, bright-colored porcelain called Kutani ware, Kenrokuen, one of Three Great Gardens of Japan, and it was also where Nyotaimori originated which is the art of of eating sushi off of a naked female. (Source: Ishikawa Official Site)
